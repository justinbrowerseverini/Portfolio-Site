InkTank

The Problem

InkTank is the solution for tattoo seekers who feel confused and overwhelmed by the magnitude of choices of Tattoo designs, styles, and artists. This app helps tattoo seekers organize their thoughts and communicate with the community around them to brainstorm the perfect tattoo while finding the right artist at a shop nearby.

InkTank is a passion project. As someone who is looking for their next tattoo, having trouble deciding what to get, and living in an area where I don’t know the quality and style of the local shops. I decided to create an app that would answer those questions.

User Interviews

The process of defining InkTank started by first conducting interviews with potential users. These interviews provided me with answers and data as to how individuals would use an app like this and what features they would look for. I then took this data and compiled it to build personas of what the average user might look like.

Personas

Those personas took life as John and Susan. The persona of John is a young professional who represents those who are familiar with the process of getting a tattoo and like to include their friends and family in their decision

Susan, as a middle-aged mother who is interested in getting a tattoo with her daughter, is a representation of those who are new to getting tattoos and would appreciate the ease of having all of the information, answers and ideas in one place.

Flow charts

Based on the need and wants of model users, John and Susan, I then decided on what features would make sense to be in InkTank and which ones would be the most commonly used, high priority ones. From that information I created flowcharts that show how a user will navigate through InkTank to complete those desired tasks.

Sketches

The next step was to start designing. I began with sketching the main pages of the app using pencil and paper. I then quickly translated those designs into low-fidelity wireframes that matched the steps of the flowcharts.

Mockups

I then upgraded the designs to a mid-fidelity mockup and conducted some user tests. The tests helped to ensure that InkTank had a simple, intuitive flow.

A few hiccups where discovered, one of the biggest issues was the placement of my search bar was causing confusion. My test participants weren't sure what type of information could be searched.

Once I received all of the feedback I was able to make corrections and InkTank was moved into a High-Fidelity mockup.

Style Guide

The Style was being finalized here and I made sure to keep a close eye making this as accessible as possible to as many people as possible. Because anyone (of age) who wants a tattoo should be able to get one, and InkTank should be available to anyone who wants a tattoo.

Visual Design

Finally, I made some last adjustments making sure everything was squared up and the design was consistent throughout. The current design uses a bold blue color to mirror the boldness of our uses and a simple design to allow our users to keep and open creative mind.

The Future of InkTank

Looking forward, I have a few ideas for updates and improvements that will continue to make InkTank more intuitive and user friendly. The ultimate plan for InkTank is to grow a community around the app and in turn allow that community to mold the future of InkTank.
